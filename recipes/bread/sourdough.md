# Sourdough at 68°F (20°C)

## Morning-to-Next-Morning Schedule

## Dough
- 450 g bread flour
- 50 g whole wheat flour
- 380 g water
- 100 g active starter
- 10 g salt

## Schedule

### 8:00 AM - Autolyse
Mix:
- 450 g bread flour
- 50 g whole wheat flour
- 360 g water

Mix until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest for 30 to 45 minutes.

### 8:45 AM - Add Starter and Salt
Add:
- 100 g active starter
- 10 g salt
- Remaining 20 g water

Mix well until the dough comes together.

### 9:15 AM - First Fold
- Do your first stretch and fold or coil fold.

### 9:45 AM - Second Fold
- Do another fold.

### 10:15 AM - Third Fold
- Do another fold.

### 10:45 AM - Fourth Fold (Optional)
- Optional if the dough still feels slack.
- If it already feels smoother and stronger, you can skip this one.

### 10:45 AM to 2:30-3:30 PM - Finish Bulk Fermentation
- Let the dough rest undisturbed.
- At 68°F (20°C), bulk will usually finish around 6 to 7 hours total from when starter was added, so start checking around 2:30 PM.

Look for:
- About 40 to 50% rise
- Dough looks puffier
- Small bubbles at the sides or top
- Slight jiggle
- Dough feels airy

### 2:30-3:30 PM - Preshape
- Turn out the dough, gently preshape, and let it rest for 20 minutes.

### 3:00-4:00 PM - Final Shape
- Shape tightly and place in a floured banneton or towel-lined bowl.

### 3:15-4:15 PM - Into the Fridge
- Cover and refrigerate overnight, about 14 to 18 hours.

## Next Morning

### 7:30-8:00 AM - Preheat Oven
- Preheat oven and Dutch oven to 475°F (246°C) for about 45 minutes.

### 8:15 AM - Bake
- Bake straight from the fridge.
- 20 minutes covered at 475°F (246°C)
- Reduce to 450°F (232°C)
- Bake 20 to 25 minutes uncovered
- Bake until deep brown.

## Cool
- Let it cool at least 1 to 2 hours before slicing.

## Notes
- At 68°F, the dough may move a little slower or faster depending on starter strength.
- Trust the dough more than the clock.
- Do not wait for the dough to double during bulk fermentation.
- If your kitchen runs cool or your starter is a bit slow, you can increase the starter to 110 to 120 g for a slightly faster schedule.
